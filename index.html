The Paper at a Glance

Self-driving cars increasingly rely on deep neural networks to achieve human-like driving. The opacity of such black-box planners makes it challenging to accurately anticipate when they will fail, with potentially catastrophic consequences. While research into interpreting these systems has surged, most of it is confined to simulations or toy setups due to the difficulty of real-world deployment, leaving the practical utility of such techniques unknown.

Here, we introduce the Concept-Wrapper Network (CW-Net), a method for faithfully explaining the behavior of machine-learning-based planners that causally grounds their reasoning in human-interpretable concepts without sacrificing performance. We deploy CW-Net on a real self-driving car and show that the resulting explanations improve the human driver’s mental model of the vehicle, allowing them to better predict its behavior, particularly in surprising situations.

Keywords: Self-driving cars • Interpretable ML • Explainable AI • Mental models • Human–computer interaction • Situational awareness

THE METHOD

CW-Net: Planning over human-friendly concepts

We start with a strong black-box ML planner trained via inverse reinforcement learning on 80 hours of human expert driving. This planner selects trajectories by estimating how human-like each candidate trajectory is.

To make it interpretable, we wrap the final reward layer with a concept classifier that predicts human-interpretable concepts such as:

  • CLOSE — Close to another vehicle
  • ASV — Approaching stopped vehicle
  • BIKE — Close to cyclist

A new reward layer then makes decisions solely from these concept probabilities. Because the final decision depends only on the concepts, the explanations are causally faithful — they directly reflect the planner’s reasoning.

REAL-WORLD VALIDATION

Mental model improvement in deployment

We deployed CW-Net on a real self-driving car on a private track using the Lab2Car wrapper. Safety drivers encountered naturally occurring surprising situations. Explanations helped them rapidly update their understanding of the vehicle.

CLOSE Concept
CLOSE TO ANOTHER VEHICLE
The AV repeatedly stopped before a pedestrian pickup/drop-off zone. The driver initially thought it was reacting to the zone itself. CW-Net revealed high CLOSE activation — it was stopping because of nearby parked vehicles. When the driver moved the car farther away, CLOSE probability dropped and the AV resumed driving.
DRIVER UPDATED MENTAL MODEL • AV BEHAVIOR MATCHED EXPLANATION
ASV Concept
APPROACHING STOPPED VEHICLE
The AV reliably came to a stop next to a traffic cone. The driver’s initial hypothesis: the cone was causing phantom braking. CW-Net showed strong ASV activation right before stopping. Even after the cone was removed in a counterfactual test, the same stopping behavior and ASV spike occurred — proving the planner was “hallucinating” a stopped vehicle from training data patterns.
CAUSALLY FAITHFUL EXPLANATION • GLOBAL ANALYSIS CONFIRMED ASV PREDICTS BRAKING
BIKE Concept
CLOSE TO CYCLIST
When approaching a cyclist, the AV always stopped safely — but the BIKE concept stayed near 0%. The driver gradually became more cautious after seeing the low concept reading. Post-hoc analysis revealed the perception system detected the cyclist, but the ML planner had no cyclist inputs configured. The AV stopped thanks to a built-in safety backup system.
DRIVER’S INCREASED CAUTION WAS APPROPRIATE • REVEALED SYSTEM LIMITATION

These three naturally occurring situations on the private track demonstrated that CW-Net explanations can correct inaccurate mental models in real time and improve the driver’s ability to predict AV behavior.

LARGE-SCALE VALIDATION

Simulation studies & situational awareness

Mental Models & Predictability

We ran online studies (experts + non-experts) using replays of the private track scenarios with overlaid CW-Net explanations. Participants completed nearest-neighbor and prediction tasks before and after seeing explanations.

  • • 8/9 experts and 27/30 non-experts improved their mental models
  • • Mental model improvement strongly correlated with better prediction accuracy
  • • Free-form explanations shifted toward ground-truth reasons after viewing CW-Net outputs

SAGAT Study (N=100)

We deployed CW-Net on public roads in Las Vegas and collected naturalistic surprising and unsurprising scenarios. Using the gold-standard SAGAT framework for situational awareness:

A deployment-validated pathway to interpretability

CW-Net shows that explainable deep learning can be both understandable and useful in realistic autonomous driving settings. By revealing the planner’s reasoning in real time through human-friendly concepts, it helps drivers build better mental models and anticipate failures before they happen.

Causally faithful explanations
No performance sacrifice
Proven in real-world deployment
The same approach can be extended to autonomous drones, robotic surgeons, and other safety-critical systems that rely on deep learning.
